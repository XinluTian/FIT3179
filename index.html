Tourism Dynamics and Economic Impact in Australia

This dashboard explores tourism consumption and its economic impact across Australian states from 1998 to 2023. It compares pre- and post-pandemic changes in tourist numbers and spending, highlighting tourism’s recovery and its influence on regional economies.

Evolution of International Tourism in Australia

In the 1990s, the main tourist sources were the United States, the United Kingdom, and Japan. By the 2000s, China became a significant contributor to Australia’s tourism, driven by its growing economy and middle class. The 2008 Global Financial Crisis caused a temporary dip, particularly in Western markets, but the Asia-Pacific region remained strong.

Tourism peaked in the late 2010s, with record numbers from China and Southeast Asia. However, the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 led to a near-total halt in international travel, as reflected by lighter shades. The industry started recovering in 2022, though numbers remain below pre-pandemic levels.

Tourism to Australia: COVID-19 Impact

From 1991 to 2019, international tourism to Australia experienced steady growth, peaking at over 20 million visitors. However, the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 led to an abrupt halt, dramatically reducing tourist numbers.

The red line highlights the onset of the pandemic, which caused a near-total collapse in travel. Though recovery efforts began in 2021, visitor numbers remain significantly below pre-pandemic levels. This sharp contrast underscores the profound effect of the pandemic on Australia’s tourism industry, marking one of the most significant downturns in decades.

Australian Tourism – Travel Purposes

This map highlights the different reasons people traveled to Australia from 1998 to 2016. Larger circles indicate a higher number of trips. Popular destinations like Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane attracted a mix of holidaymakers, business travelers, and other visitors.

You can explore how these patterns change over time using the year selector and observe how different regions attract visitors for different purposes. Discover Australia’s top spots for business trips, vacations, and more across the years.

Tourism's Contribution to Australia's Economy by State

In 2022-23, tourism played a crucial role in several Australian states, especially in regions like Queensland and Tasmania, where it made up a significant portion of the economy.

This chart shows how tourism, represented in pink and green, contributed both directly and indirectly to each state’s economic activities. In comparison, states like Western Australia and the Northern Territory relied more heavily on industries such as mining and construction, with a smaller portion attributed to tourism.

The chart clearly illustrates how the tourism industry interacts with other key sectors, shaping Australia's economic landscape across regions.

Between 2007 and 2023, tourism consumption in Australian states experienced significant shifts. By using the interactive slider, you can select specific time periods to view how tourism spending has changed year by year, or focus on average consumption over a selected period.

For example, selecting 2010-2015 shows the average tourism consumption steadily increasing across most states, with New South Wales and Queensland consistently leading in total spending. The COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 marks a sharp decline, but the chart allows you to easily compare recovery trends across states.
